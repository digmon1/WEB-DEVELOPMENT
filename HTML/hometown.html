Bansgadhi Municipality (Nepali: बाँसगढी नगरपालिका) is a local government unit in the Bardiya District of Lumbini Province in southwestern Nepal. Established on December 2, 2014, it was formed by merging the former Village Development Committees of Beluwa, Motipur, and Deudhakola. The municipality covers a total area of 206 square kilometers and is situated in the Terai region, approximately 20 kilometers north of Nepalganj along the Mahendra Highway. It is bordered by the Gyang River and Barbardiya Municipality to the west, Bardiya National Park to the north, and the districts of Banke and Salyan to the east and northeast.
